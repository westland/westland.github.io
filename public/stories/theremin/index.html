Theremin

Léon Theremin led a rich and interesting life. A brilliant scientist and darling of Lenin and the Soviet State, he defected to the US in 1927 on a Soviet technology tour; became a New York and Hollywood music celebrity; married a young African-American ballerina, Lavinia Williams – scandalous in those days; and was later kidnapped by KGB agents in New York and spirited back to the Soviet Union. His most famous invention was the ‘theremin’, the first electronic musical instrument; his second most famous invention was a listening device, ‘the Thing’, that hung for seven years in plain view in the US Ambassador’s Moscow office and enabled Soviet agents to eavesdrop on secret conversations.

His American admirer, Bob Moog, started his eponymous musical instrument company building solid-state theremins. Moog was obsessed with finding out what had happened to Theremin, and in 1990 traveled to the Soviet Union to learn specifics, where, and when Theremin might have lived and died. One day while meeting with an academic in Moscow, he brought up the subject of Leon Theremin and what might have happened to him. “Oh” his companion remarked “I had lunch with him just yesterday.” Moog and the academic went out later that day to track down 95 year old Theremin, bringing him back to the US to much acclaim in 1991.
