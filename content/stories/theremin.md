Léon Theremin led a rich and interesting life. A bril- liant scientist and darling of Lenin and the Soviet State, he defected to the US in 1927 on a Soviet technology tour; became a New York and Hollywood music celebrity; married a young African-American ballerina, Lavinia Williams – scandalous in those days; and was later kidnapped by KGB agents in New York and spirited back to the Soviet Union. His most famous invention was the ‘theremin’, the first electronic musical instrument; his second most famous invention was a listening device, ‘the Thing’, that hung for seven years in plain view in the US Ambassador’s Moscow office and enabled Soviet agents to eavesdrop on secret conversations. His American admirer, Bob Moog, brought Theremin back to the US to much acclaim in 1991 when he was 95 years old.
