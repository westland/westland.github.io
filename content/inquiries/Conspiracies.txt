10,000 years ago, societies of [~150](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunbar%27s_number) hunted, gathered and subsistence farmed, and at the end of the day, sat around the firepit exchanging stories. Everyone could contribute to this egalitarian contest of fabulist stories, the most entertaining conferring status and gaining legitimacy.  Science, law, ethics thus gleaned were unreliable and fluid, captive to a [Gossip Trap](https://theconversation.com/dunbars-number-why-my-theory-that-humans-can-only-maintain-150-friendships-has-withstood-30-years-of-scrutiny-160676).  Egyptian priests melded stories with nilometers to predict crop yield, and elaborate cosmologies to predict heavenly events.  These driblets of science allowed them to grow rich and powerful, fielding religious orders, armies and construction crews.  Priests were the curators and interpreters of news, law, science, and morality in service of royalty, and broadcast to the people through thier temples.  And so it was for millenia as religions proliferated, morphing into preisthoods of science and law.  Printing, telegraph, radio and TV were the [new Gods of technology](https://www.amazon.com/American-Gods-Neil-Gaiman/dp/0380973650/ref=asc_df_0380973650/?tag=hyprod-20&linkCode=df0&hvadid=312114918153&hvpos=&hvnetw=g&hvrand=14977556065872423235&hvpone=&hvptwo=&hvqmt=&hvdev=c&hvdvcmdl=&hvlocint=&hvlocphy=9029978&hvtargid=pla-432992956489&psc=1&tag=&ref=&adgrpid=58874700661&hvpone=&hvptwo=&hvadid=312114918153&hvpos=&hvnetw=g&hvrand=14977556065872423235&hvqmt=&hvdev=c&hvdvcmdl=&hvlocint=&hvlocphy=9029978&hvtargid=pla-432992956489&gclid=Cj0KCQiAj_CrBhD-ARIsAIiMxT-XHLTqXOsT14Fe8V3MQJwXzUGaGOXv28CTwfDOtML8lgJSjChpSd4aAt0BEALw_wcB), and information flowed only one direction -- from curators to the people.  All was well (so it seemed).

And then the old world order was challenged.  Exponential accelaration in bandwidth [tripling every 9 months claimed George Gilder](https://www.amazon.com/Telecosm-World-After-Bandwidth-Abundance/dp/0743205472/ref=asc_df_0743205472/?tag=hyprod-20&linkCode=df0&hvadid=312114711253&hvpos=&hvnetw=g&hvrand=1902536072246505003&hvpone=&hvptwo=&hvqmt=&hvdev=c&hvdvcmdl=&hvlocint=&hvlocphy=9031623&hvtargid=pla-567735725442&psc=1&mcid=3208fbaa77eb3c87a95fcc99acb2433f&gclid=CjwKCAiA-bmsBhAGEiwAoaQNmgkEH7_RboKBP_vVcU2yToi7N9uV75DyGlhg0D-wlzihnB70WP0vsBoC598QAvD_BwE) made interactive 'social' media a reality.  Faceook, Twitter, TikTok competing for content and attention became platforms for a Gossip Trap redux.  Like egalitarian societies of old, everyone was encouraged to exchange fabulist stories.  Conspiracy theories proliferated.  Recipes for a tasty, sticky influential conspiracy theory require a pinch of truth, a pinch of emotion, a pinch of threat, and a large dollop of entertainment.  A good conspiricy is nothing without a kernal of truth -- a compelling mystery, an evasive explanation by authorities, and situation where truth is too complex to comprehend, so a dishonest truth is the next best choice  Most importantly: they need to be simple, communicated only in sound bites.  To qualify as conspiracies, they probably need also to upend received wisdom in science, politics, religion and challenge authority.  And just like that, we've returned to a world of competing narratives.  

Lizard people, Illuminati, Bilderbergs,urban legends jocky for our attention.  Why do we devote so much of our time and attention to alledged global movements that have no impact on our daily lives, and where we would have next to zero influence?  Maybe these are constructed of the same substance as origin myths, theologies and local gossip.  Maybe they are a low consequence strategy in the competition for revenge and social status.

Scratch the surface of popular conspiracies, and you find religion and fabulist stories.  Conspiracy theories are made of the same stuff as faith and superstition, and demand Orwellian talents of "doublethink",  "thoughtcrime", and "2 + 2 = 5".
